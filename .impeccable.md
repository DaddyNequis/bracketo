## Design Context

### Users
Athletes and tournament participants who compete at **live in-person events** (esports, tennis, badminton, soccer, fighting games). They use the bracket during the event — on a screen at the venue or on a personal device. They are physically active, competitive, and emotionally invested in the outcome. Secondary audience: **tournament operators** who manage scores in real-time under pressure. Tertiary: **developers** who evaluate the library by exploring the demo.

### Brand Personality
**Energetic · Athletic · Competitive**

The interface should feel like a sports performance app — Strava, Nike Run Club, Apple Fitness. Intense but not aggressive. It should make athletes feel their competition is *serious* and *worth showing off*.

Emotional goals: pride, excitement, competitive drive. The bracket is not an admin tool — it's a stage.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Color**: Green as primary accent — not neon, but athletic green (think Strava's `#FC4C02`-energy but in green — e.g., `oklch(65% 0.22 145)` — vivid, confident, saturated). Dark surfaces to make green pop. Avoid generic Tailwind blue-500.
- **Typography**: A distinctive display font for headings and match labels (something with athletic weight — condensed, bold). Clean sans for data.
- **Tone**: High-contrast, high-energy, but readable. The bracket is a *scoreboard*, not a dashboard.
- **References**: Strava app, ESPN bracket view, Nike Training Club. NOT: generic SaaS dark dashboards, Vercel/Linear-style neutral minimalism.
- **Anti-references**: Pure neon cyberpunk, generic AI navy+blue palette, corporate dashboard aesthetic.
- **Theme**: Dark first, green accent. Light theme optional (consumer can override tokens).

### Design Principles
1. **The bracket is the hero** — every other UI element serves it. Sidebar, toolbar, modals are supporting cast.
2. **Clarity under pressure** — operators edit scores live; athletes check standings in excitement. Everything must be instantly readable at a glance.
3. **Athletic identity** — use visual language from sports design: bold type, strong contrast, purposeful color, motion that feels physical.
4. **Zero-friction editing** — score entry must be discoverable, fast, and forgiving. No multi-step workflows without clear signposting.
5. **Ownable defaults** — the library's default theme should be distinctly *Bracketo*, not a generic dark UI any developer could have written.
